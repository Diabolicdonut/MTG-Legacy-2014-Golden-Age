1 · Deck identity

What the archetype is

Food Chain played a real midrange game with mana creatures, Agents, Strixes, and countermagic. Once Food Chain resolved, Misthollow Griffin became reusable mana and the deck could cast a game-ending creature.

2 · History

Evolution during the Golden Era

2012

Misthollow Griffin gave Food Chain a resilient exile-zone engine.

2013

Deathrite Shaman, Baleful Strix, and Shardless Agent strengthened the fair plan.

2014

Manipulate Fate lists refined the balance between combo density and BUG interaction.

5 · How to pilot

Core decisions

1. Present ordinary BUG pressure until the opponent taps low enough for Food Chain.

2. Do not expose every Griffin to graveyard interaction when exile is the safer zone.

6 · Sideboarding

Period tools

  • Discard and Flusterstorm for combo
  • Submerge for green creature decks
  • Null Rod for artifacts
  • Graveyard hate and additional value creatures
7 · Matchup guide

The major opponents

OpponentPlan
MiraclesPressure its engine without overcommitting into Terminus; preserve answers to Counterbalance and Jace.
Delver variantsRespect Daze and Wasteland, lower the curve after sideboarding, and protect the mana needed for the deck’s defining engine.
Death & TaxesIdentify whether Vial, Thalia, equipment, or mana denial is the permanent that must be answered first.
Storm and Show and TellReplace slow fair cards with discard, counters, or hate and pair interaction with a real clock.
Graveyard decksMulligan toward graveyard interaction and avoid giving unlimited draw steps after the first answer.
