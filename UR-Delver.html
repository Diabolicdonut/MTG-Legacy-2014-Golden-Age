1 · Deck identity

UR Delver

UR Delver uses Delver, Young Pyromancer, and sometimes True-Name or Lavamancer to translate cheap spells into pressure. Without green creatures or white removal, it relies on speed, token production, and direct damage.

1–2 mana
Primary operating range

Narrow margin

2 · What this deck does

The operating plan

Engine

The leanest blue-red tempo shell: cheap threats, cantrips, free counters, and enough burn to make every life point matter.

Core tension

The archetype must balance its defining engine against speed, mana stability, and the interaction required by an unusually broad Legacy field.

Winning resource

The deck succeeds when its signature cards create more mana, cards, tempo, or inevitability than the opponent can answer efficiently.

Primary weakness

Opponents attack the engine through mana denial, stack interaction, permanent hate, sweepers, or graveyard disruption depending on the matchup.

5 · Mulligans and opening turns

Keep a functional sequence

Strong keeps

  • A cheap threat, functional mana, and at least one piece of interaction.
  • A one-land hand only when cantrips and matchup speed make it credible.
  • A hand whose disruption matches the opponent rather than merely looking efficient.

Common mulligans

  • Reactive hands with no clock.
  • Color-fragile hands that lose to one Wasteland.
  • Hands overloaded with expensive threats or situational counters.
Opening principle. A hand should explain what the deck does on turns one, two, and three—and what happens if its first engine card is answered.

8 · Sideboarding principles

Preserve the deck while changing the fight

Structural principles

  • Trim soft counters when the opponent naturally plays a long game.
  • Keep enough threats after sideboarding to convert interaction into a win.
  • Exchange generic permission for matchup-specific one-mana answers.

Period sideboard tools

  • Pyroblast for blue decks
  • Artifact destruction for equipment and Chalice
  • Surgical effects for graveyard combo
  • Sweepers for opposing token and tribal boards
