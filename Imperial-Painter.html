1 · Deck identity

Imperial Painter

Imperial Painter attacks mana with Moon effects and the library with Painter plus Grindstone. Recruiter turns the creature package into a toolbox, while Pyroblast becomes a universal answer after Painter names blue.

2 · What this deck does

The operating plan

Engine

A mono-red Blood Moon prison deck with an instant-kill Painter’s Servant and Grindstone combo assembled by Imperial Recruiter.

Core tension

The archetype must balance its defining engine against speed, mana stability, and the interaction required by an unusually broad Legacy field.

Winning resource

The deck succeeds when its signature cards create more mana, cards, tempo, or inevitability than the opponent can answer efficiently.

Primary weakness

Opponents attack the engine through mana denial, stack interaction, permanent hate, sweepers, or graveyard disruption depending on the matchup.

5 · Mulligans and opening turns

Keep a functional sequence

Strong keeps

  • Fast mana plus a lock piece relevant to the matchup.
  • A hand that deploys pressure after disruption.
  • Redundant mana that survives one Wasteland or Force.

Common mulligans

  • Fast mana with no meaningful payoff.
  • A lock piece that does not affect the known opponent.
  • Hands that collapse if the first spell is countered.
Opening principle. A hand should explain what the deck does on turns one, two, and three—and what happens if its first engine card is answered.

8 · Sideboarding principles

Preserve the deck while changing the fight

Structural principles

  • Replace dead prison pieces with targeted answers.
  • Keep enough threats to close before the opponent recovers.
  • Diversify permanents against narrow removal.

Period sideboard tools

  • Crypt effects for Emrakul and graveyards
  • Extra Bridges for creatures
  • Vandal effects for artifacts
  • Koth and alternate threats against removal-heavy control
9 · Gauntlet-specific matchup guide

The major pre-Cruise opponents

MatchupRolePlan
MiraclesLock + clockChoose the lock piece that attacks their mana or cheap spells and deploy pressure immediately.
RUG DelverLock + clockA resolved lock can win, but Wasteland and Force punish fragile mana. Keep redundant acceleration.
Death & TaxesLock + clockThe game becomes a permanent and mana battle; dead lock pieces should become targeted answers.
Shardless BUGLock + clockPrevent their efficient answers from trading cleanly and win before planeswalkers take over.
ANT / TESLock + clockFast Chalice, Thalia, Moon, or Trinisphere effects are the entire matchup; mulligan accordingly.
Sneak / OmniLock + clockLock their cantrips or mana and use Revoker/Needle-style effects on the engine.
ElvesLock + clockBoard control and sweepers matter more than spell taxes alone.
Reanimator / DredgeLock + clockGeneric prison is insufficient without graveyard hate; pair both with a clock.
