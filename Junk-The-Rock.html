1 · Deck identity

What the archetype is

Junk—often called The Rock—used the best standalone cards in black, green, and white. It traded blue cantrips for discard, resilient creatures, and broad permanent answers.

2 · History

Evolution during the Golden Era

2012

BGW Junk appeared as a meaningful Legacy Championship archetype.

2013

Deathrite Shaman and Abrupt Decay modernized the shell.

2014

The deck overlapped with Maverick and Jund but retained its own discard-heavy identity.

5 · How to pilot

2. Sequence basics and duals around Wasteland while preserving double-black spells.

6 · Sideboarding

Period tools

  • Gaddock Teeg for combo and Miracles
  • Zealous Persecution for small creatures
  • Surgical for graveyards
  • Choke for blue control
7 · Matchup guide

The major opponents

OpponentPlan
MiraclesPressure its engine without overcommitting into Terminus; preserve answers to Counterbalance and Jace.
Delver variantsRespect Daze and Wasteland, lower the curve after sideboarding, and protect the mana needed for the deck’s defining engine.
Death & TaxesIdentify whether Vial, Thalia, equipment, or mana denial is the permanent that must be answered first.
Storm and Show and TellReplace slow fair cards with discard, counters, or hate and pair interaction with a real clock.
Graveyard decksMulligan toward graveyard interaction and avoid giving unlimited draw steps after the first answer.
