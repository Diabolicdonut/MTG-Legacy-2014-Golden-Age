1 · Deck identity

LED Dredge

LED Dredge uses discard and draw spells to replace normal draws with dredges. Narcomoeba and Ichorid enter for free, Bridge creates Zombies, Therapy strips interaction, and Dread Return converts creatures into a finishing target.

2 · What this deck does

The operating plan

Engine

Turn the graveyard into a hand, battlefield, sacrifice engine, and combo kill through dredge, Ichorid, Bridge, Therapy, and Dread Return.

Core tension

The archetype must balance its defining engine against speed, mana stability, and the interaction required by an unusually broad Legacy field.

Winning resource

The deck succeeds when its signature cards create more mana, cards, tempo, or inevitability than the opponent can answer efficiently.

Primary weakness

Opponents attack the engine through mana denial, stack interaction, permanent hate, sweepers, or graveyard disruption depending on the matchup.

5 · Mulligans and opening turns

Keep a functional sequence

Strong keeps

  • An outlet, a payoff, and enough velocity.
  • A hand that can act before common hate becomes active.
  • Redundancy against one counter or graveyard activation.

Common mulligans

  • Payoffs with no way to place them in the graveyard.
  • Hands that lose to one obvious hate permanent without recourse.
  • Slow hands against combo or prison.
Opening principle. A hand should explain what the deck does on turns one, two, and three—and what happens if its first engine card is answered.

8 · Sideboarding principles

Preserve the deck while changing the fight

Structural principles

  • Board minimally to preserve engine density.
  • Answer the hate card the opponent can deploy, not every theoretical card.
  • Use transformational plans when the graveyard will be over-defended.

Period sideboard tools

  • Nature’s Claim for permanent hate
  • Chain of Vapor for flexible bounce
  • Firestorm against creatures and hatebears
  • Alternate Dread Return targets for specific boards
