1 · Deck identity

Omni-Tell

Omni-Tell is the pure spell-combo branch of Show and Tell. Unlike Sneak and Show, it does not need red mana after the enabler and can win immediately through Enter the Infinite, Cunning Wish, and free protection.

2 · What this deck does

The operating plan

Engine

Resolve Show and Tell for Omniscience, cast Enter the Infinite, and finish through Emrakul or a Cunning Wish line.

Core tension

The archetype must balance its defining engine against speed, mana stability, and the interaction required by an unusually broad Legacy field.

Winning resource

The deck succeeds when its signature cards create more mana, cards, tempo, or inevitability than the opponent can answer efficiently.

Primary weakness

Opponents attack the engine through mana denial, stack interaction, permanent hate, sweepers, or graveyard disruption depending on the matchup.

5 · Mulligans and opening turns

Keep a functional sequence

Strong keeps

  • A plausible route to a kill plus enough mana.
  • Cantrips and protection when the matchup allows time.
  • A redundant hand that survives one counter or discard spell.

Common mulligans

  • Mana without a payoff.
  • Payoffs without an enabler.
  • Slow hands against a faster known opponent.
Opening principle. A hand should explain what the deck does on turns one, two, and three—and what happens if its first engine card is answered.

7 · How to pilot the deck

Convert the engine into a win

The cards define the archetype; sequencing determines whether those cards become a coherent strategy.

1. Cantrip toward an enabler, payoff, and protection rather than collecting redundant pieces

2. Against permanent hate, plan the exact Wish or bounce line before resolving Show and Tell

3. Preserve blue cards for Force and Pacts

4. Do not cast Enter the Infinite without a deterministic finish

5. Use Sol lands only when acceleration outweighs Wasteland exposure

6. Against combo, a fast Show and Tell can be the correct defensive play

8 · Sideboarding principles

Preserve the deck while changing the fight

Structural principles

  • Do not dilute the combo with too many answers.
  • Bring interaction for the hate that actually stops the deck.
  • Change routes when the opponent overcommits to one form of defense.

Period sideboard tools

  • Defense Grid for blue decks
  • Wish bullets for bounce and counters
  • Cage for graveyard decks
  • Pyroclasm for hatebear swarms
