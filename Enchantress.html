1 · Deck identity

Enchantress

Enchantress is a permanent-based draw engine. Argothian and Presence make cheap mana auras replace themselves; Sterling Grove protects and tutors; Confinement converts card flow into a lock; Replenish rebuilds after sweepers.

2 · What this deck does

The operating plan

Engine

Turn every enchantment into another card, lock combat with Elephant Grass and Solitary Confinement, then win through a protected inevitability engine.

Core tension

The archetype must balance its defining engine against speed, mana stability, and the interaction required by an unusually broad Legacy field.

Winning resource

The deck succeeds when its signature cards create more mana, cards, tempo, or inevitability than the opponent can answer efficiently.

Primary weakness

Opponents attack the engine through mana denial, stack interaction, permanent hate, sweepers, or graveyard disruption depending on the matchup.

5 · Mulligans and opening turns

Keep a functional sequence

Strong keeps

  • Mana, an engine piece, and a bridge to the middle game.
  • A tutor hand whose first target solves a real problem.
  • A hand resilient to one removal or counterspell.

Common mulligans

  • Pieces that do nothing without the missing engine.
  • Hands with powerful cards but no functional curve.
  • Hands that fold to the matchup’s most common hate card.
Opening principle. A hand should explain what the deck does on turns one, two, and three—and what happens if its first engine card is answered.

7 · How to pilot the deck

The cards define the archetype; sequencing determines whether those cards become a coherent strategy.

8 · Sideboarding principles

Preserve the deck while changing the fight

Structural principles

  • Keep engine density high.
  • Replace the weakest tutors or bullets with targeted interaction.
  • Use alternative win conditions against surgical hate.

Period sideboard tools

  • Leyline for discard and Tendrils
  • Carpet and Choke for blue decks
  • Rest in Peace for graveyards
  • Stony Silence, Halo, and Humility as silver bullets
9 · Gauntlet-specific matchup guide

The major pre-Cruise opponents

MatchupRolePlan
MiraclesControl / inevitabilityFight over engines and planeswalkers, make land drops, and avoid committing everything into a sweeper.
RUG DelverControl / inevitabilityLower the curve, stabilize mana, and trade early; the late game is favorable only if lands survive.
Death & TaxesControl / inevitabilityAnswer Vial, equipment, or lock pieces before they invalidate expensive cards.
Shardless BUGControl / inevitabilityCard advantage engines matter most; preserve the final threat or planeswalker after exchanges.
ANT / TESControl / inevitabilitySideboard heavily, establish a clock, and use discard or counters on the spell that cannot be answered later.
Sneak / OmniControl / inevitabilityKeep interaction for the enabler and retain a permanent answer when possible.
ElvesControl / inevitabilityStop the engine rather than every creature, and use sweepers at the highest-leverage moment.
Reanimator / DredgeControl / inevitabilityMulligan to graveyard interaction and pair it with pressure before they find an answer.
